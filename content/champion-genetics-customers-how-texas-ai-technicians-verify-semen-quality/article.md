## Laboratory verification at Champion Genetics
Champion follows NAAB/CSS protocols for every collection. After collection, technicians assess volume, color, and consistency, then evaluate the sample under a microscope for concentration, motility, and morphology. An extender with egg yolk and glycerol is added to protect sperm during freezing.
Collections that meet internal standards proceed to straw filling and controlled freezing in liquid nitrogen. Champion performs additional post-thaw quality assurance on every batch, including post-thaw motility, incubated motility, and morphology. Only semen meeting these standards is released or stored.
[img2]
For international or resale semen, full CSS certification applies. This requires extended bull quarantine and testing for tuberculosis, brucellosis, leptospirosis, BVDV, trichomoniasis, and other pathogens, along with strict identification and record-keeping. Texas regulations require a negative trichomoniasis PCR test within 60 days for breeding bulls entering the state, with exemptions for qualifying CSS-certified movements.
## Field verification by Texas AI technicians
Once frozen semen arrives on the ranch, the responsibility shifts to the AI technician. Champion-trained technicians and graduates of the company’s AI School follow a consistent sequence to maintain viability.
The standard thaw uses a 94–98°F (35–37°C) water bath for approximately 35 seconds. Technicians dry the straw, load it into a pre-warmed AI gun, and complete insemination within 15 minutes. They avoid thermal shock by keeping unused straws below the frost line and thawing only the number needed immediately.
Many technicians perform a quick motility check under a portable microscope on the first thawed straw before proceeding with the session. This on-the-spot verification helps identify any issues caused by shipping or tank handling. Proper hygiene, correct placement in the uterine body, and accurate timing relative to heat detection complete the process.
Even with strong laboratory standards, field execution can vary. Many technicians now use a simple, standardized reference to ensure nothing is missed during these critical minutes on the ranch.

## Common field mistakes that reduce conception rates
Technicians and producers report that most AI failures stem from handling issues rather than the semen itself. Frequent mistakes include:
- Thawing too many straws at once
- Allowing straw temperature to rise above 98°F during handling
- Skipping the quick motility check on the first straw
- Delaying insemination more than 15 minutes after thawing
- Poor tank management during transport or on the ranch
These errors can irreversibly damage sperm even when the semen left the lab in excellent condition. A standardized checklist helps prevent these issues on every breeding.
